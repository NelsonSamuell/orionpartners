ISO 27001 for Kenyan SMEs: where to start when you can't do everything at once

CybersecurityJuly 2026·7 min read

Full certification can feel out of reach for a small team. It doesn't have to be. ISO 27001 is a management system, not a shopping list — and for most Kenyan SMEs the sensible path is to implement the controls that reduce the most risk for the least cost first, and sequence the rest around your obligations under Kenya's Data Protection Act, 2019.

Start with the register, not the firewall

The standard's real engine is the risk assessment. Before buying anything, list what you actually hold: customer records, M-Pesa and bank integration credentials, payroll data, source code. If you process personal data, this same exercise feeds the records of processing the Data Protection Act expects — one piece of work, two obligations discharged.

The controls that pay for themselves first

  • Access control and offboarding. Shared admin passwords and ex-staff accounts that still work are behind a remarkable share of Kenyan SME incidents. Named accounts, multi-factor authentication on email and finance systems, and a same-day leaver checklist cost almost nothing.
  • Backups you have actually restored. Ransomware turns a technical problem into an existential one. A tested, offline or off-tenant backup is the single cheapest control against the most expensive incident.
  • Patching and endpoint hygiene. Automatic updates and supported operating systems close the vulnerabilities commodity attacks scan for.
  • Staff awareness. Most breaches start in an inbox. Short, recurring phishing simulations outperform an annual lecture.

What can wait

Formal supplier audits, elaborate cryptographic policies, and full business-continuity exercises matter — but they protect against rarer scenarios than a stolen password does. Sequence them into year two, once the fundamentals are routine and documented.

The Data Protection Act is your sequencing guide

Registration with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner, a breach-notification procedure (72 hours), and a basis for every category of personal data you process are legal obligations now, not certification niceties. Aligning your ISO 27001 statement of applicability with these requirements means every shilling spent counts twice.

When certification becomes worth it

Pursue the certificate when a tender or enterprise client demands it. Until then, an honestly implemented subset of the standard protects you better than a rushed, paper-only certification — and puts you eighteen months ahead when the audit does come.
